Rosie

Roseanne Park, professionally known as Rosé, is a South Korean-New Zealand singer and dancer best known as a member of the global K-pop sensation BLACKPINK. Born on February 11, 1997, in Auckland, New Zealand, and raised in Melbourne, Australia, Rosé joined YG Entertainment after a successful audition in 2012. Renowned for her unique, soulful voice and emotive performances, she quickly became one of the most distinctive artists in the industry.

Debuting with BLACKPINK in 2016, Rosé has contributed to the groups massive success with hits like “DDU-DU DDU-DU” and “How You Like That.” In 2021, she made her solo debut with the single album R, featuring the record-breaking song “On The Ground.” Beyond her music, Rosé is admired for her elegance, humility, and global influence as a fashion icon and ambassador for luxury brands such as Saint Laurent and Tiffany & Co.
